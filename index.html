Meet Erin Knapp

I've been obsessed with computers and information technology for as long as I can remember. This love began in the 80s when I stole every moment I could to use the family computer (a 386 of course) and explore its inner workings--both figuratively and literally. Growing up in the 80s and 90s, I was always the girl who know how to perform every function on a computer and whom everyone came to for help. Unfortunately in college, the sheer lack of women in computer science classes completely intimdated and overwhelmed me and I wound up getting two degrees in completely unrelated fields (B.S. Archaeological Sciences and B.A. Classics and Ancient Mediterranean Studies) and a masters degree in Molecular Anthropology.

Towards the end of my masters program I realized that my true passion was in computers and programming. I have since spent every day learning web developement through free and paid services such as: Code Academy, Treehouse, Code School, and Thinkful. I am now enrolled in a B.S. in Computer Science Engineering at FAU.

When I am not programming I am learning about programming and new technologies. My other hobbies include: playing with my Shiba Inu and yelling at my cat, watching comic book movies/TV shows, reading comic books, playing video games such as Final Fantasy 14, reading science fiction and fantasy novels, drawing/painting, photography, gardening, and sleeping. I also have a chronic illness called fibromyalgia, which causes widespread pain, fatigue, depression, anxiety, and insomnia among other things. Every day is a struggle but I refuse to give up.
